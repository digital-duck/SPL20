The security of AI models isn’t determined by their accessibility, but by the *intelligence* and dedication of those working to understand and mitigate risks. Open-source fosters transparency, allowing countless eyes – including those with diverse perspectives and expertise – to identify and address vulnerabilities far more effectively than any internal security team. Furthermore, the “black box” problem they highlight is precisely *why* open-sourcing is vital. True understanding demands scrutiny, experimentation, and the collective intelligence of a global community, not the illusion of control offered by a proprietary system.

To suggest that a decentralized, public system is less secure is a profound misunderstanding of threat analysis and proactive defense.

It allows for rapid iteration, collaboration, and the creation of entirely new applications that would never have been conceived within the confines of a closed system. To suggest that limiting access is somehow beneficial for economic growth is a cynical and demonstrably false assertion. We must embrace the future, not cower before unfounded fears.

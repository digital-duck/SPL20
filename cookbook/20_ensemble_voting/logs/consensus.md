### Consensus Summary

- **Demand-Pull Inflation:**
  - Increased consumer spending due to higher income, tax cuts, or optimistic economic conditions.
  - Government spending increases can drive up aggregate demand and prices.
  - Rising household wealth and business investment also contribute.

- **Cost-Push Inflation:**
  - Higher costs of raw materials (e.g., oil, metals).
  - Wage increases driven by union demands or labor shortages.
  - Supply chain disruptions due to natural disasters or geopolitical events.
  - Increased taxes passed on to consumers.

- **Monetary Inflation:**
  - Excessive money supply growth from central banks through quantitative easing or low interest rates.
  - Low interest rates encouraging borrowing and spending.
  - Quantity theory of money suggesting direct proportionality between money supply growth and inflation.

- **Built-in Inflation (Expectations):**
  - Workers demanding higher wages to maintain purchasing power.
  - Businesses preemptively raising prices, creating a self-fulfilling cycle.

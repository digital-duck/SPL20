**Key Challenges & Solutions:**

*   **Coordination:** The biggest hurdle isn’t compute power, but coordinating a network of dynamically joining, heterogeneous nodes. This requires a new protocol, analogous to BitTorrent or SMTP, that's open, lightweight, and economically incentivized.
*   **Thermodynamics:** Distributed nodes avoid the massive energy inefficiency of large data centers due to passive cooling and lack of overhead.
*   **Idle Energy:**  A significant amount of energy is wasted by devices constantly running in standby mode. Distributed inference can utilize this idle energy.
*   **Carbon Intensity:**  Geographically distributed nodes can reduce overall carbon intensity by leveraging the average grid carbon intensity of participating regions, particularly as renewable energy penetration increases.

**Economic Models:**

*   **Incentive Design:**  Voluntary participation requires sophisticated incentive mechanisms (like token-based rewards) to prevent the “tragedy of the commons.”
*   **Enterprise Adoption:**  The primary value proposition for businesses is resilience and overflow capacity, not just cost savings.

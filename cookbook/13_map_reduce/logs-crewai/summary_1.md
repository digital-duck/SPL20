This section explores the critical challenges and solutions surrounding distributed AI inference, specifically addressing energy considerations and economic models. Key points include: the “coordination problem” – the lack of standardized infrastructure for managing heterogeneous, voluntary participation in distributed inference; a thermodynamic argument favoring distributed nodes that avoid data center cooling inefficiencies; the significant impact of idle device energy consumption; and the need for economic incentives (token-based reward systems) to mitigate the tragedy of the commons within a distributed inference network, mirroring successful models like frequent-flier miles or cloud compute credits.

The core challenge in distributed AI inference lies not in compute, but in coordination: matching requests, maintaining quality, handling failures, and incentivizing participation. Traditional service meshes are unsuitable for the dynamic, heterogeneous nature of voluntary, consumer-based networks. A novel coordination protocol, analogous to BitTorrent or SMTP, is needed—open, lightweight, and economically rational. This approach addresses thermodynamic inefficiencies of centralized data centers, leveraging idle device energy and mitigating carbon intensity through geographic distribution. Token-based incentive mechanisms, resembling frequent-flier miles, offer a viable economic model for enterprise adoption, prioritizing resilience and overflow capacity over simple cost reduction.

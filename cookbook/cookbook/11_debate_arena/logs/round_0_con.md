My opponent's assertion that AI should be open-sourced relies on a flawed assumption: that transparency and collaboration can outweigh the numerous risks associated with releasing proprietary technology. This would undermine the very fabric of our economy, stifling innovation and growth. The consequences would be severe: companies would be forced to invest heavily in developing alternative solutions, diverting resources from other critical areas. The resulting economic instability would have far-reaching consequences, affecting not only individual businesses but also entire industries.

In reality, releasing sensitive information without proper clearance could compromise the very integrity of our intelligence agencies' operations. By keeping AI source code proprietary, we can ensure that sensitive information remains out of the hands of unauthorized actors.

In conclusion, my opponent's assertion that AI should be open-sourced is a misguided one, driven by an overly optimistic view of human nature and technology. The risks associated with releasing proprietary AI technology far outweigh any perceived benefits, and I firmly believe that we must prioritize intellectual property protection to ensure the continued growth and innovation of our global economy.

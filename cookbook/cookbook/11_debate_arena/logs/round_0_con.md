Moreover, even with bug bounty programs and regular audits, the vast majority of vulnerabilities remain unaddressed due to the sheer complexity of AI systems. The introduction of open-sourced AI code increases the attack surface exponentially, making it a ticking time bomb waiting to be exploited.

Many critical technologies, such as nuclear power and space exploration, were developed behind closed doors due to national security concerns. The lack of transparency and accountability in these fields has allowed governments to maintain control over sensitive information while advancing our understanding of the world. AI systems are no exception; they require secrecy to remain competitive and drive innovation forward.

In contrast, open-sourcing AI would create a Wild West of unregulated experimentation, where intellectual property rights are trampled upon in favor of the "greater good." This approach disregards the importance of research funding and grants, which are often conditional on proprietary access to cutting-edge technologies. By opening up AI code, we risk stifling innovation in critical areas like medicine and national security. The benefits of open-sourcing AI do not outweigh the risks; instead, they are a false promise that ignores the very real concerns about security and intellectual property.

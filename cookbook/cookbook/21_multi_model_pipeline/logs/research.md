**I. The Science – What’s Happening?**

* **Greenhouse Effect:** The Earth’s atmosphere contains greenhouse gases (GHGs) – primarily carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O) – which trap heat and warm the planet. This is a natural process, but human activities have dramatically increased GHG concentrations.
* **Human Contribution:**  Since the Industrial Revolution (around 1750), human activities – primarily burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas) for energy, agriculture, and deforestation – have released unprecedented amounts of GHGs into the atmosphere.
* **Observed Changes:**  The planet is unequivocally warming, and the vast majority of this warming is attributable to human activity. Key observed changes include:
    * **Rising Global Temperatures:** The Earth’s average surface temperature has risen by about 1.1°C (2.0°F) since the pre-industrial era (1850-1900).  2023 is on track to be the warmest year on record.
    * **Sea Level Rise:**  Melting glaciers and ice sheets, along with thermal expansion of water, are causing global sea levels to rise at an accelerating rate.  Average sea level rise has been about 21-23 cm (8-9 inches) since 1880.
    * **Melting Ice:** Arctic sea ice extent has declined by about 13% per decade since 1979. Greenland and Antarctic ice sheets are losing mass at an alarming rate.
    * **Extreme Weather Events:**  There’s growing evidence that climate change is intensifying extreme weather events like heatwaves, droughts, floods, and wildfires.  The frequency and/or severity of these events are increasing in many regions.
    * **Ocean Acidification:**  The ocean absorbs roughly 30% of the CO2 emitted into the atmosphere, leading to increased acidity which threatens marine life, particularly shellfish and coral.

**II. Key Statistics & Projections**

* **CO2 Levels:** Atmospheric CO2 concentrations are currently over 420 parts per million (ppm) – the highest level in at least 800,000 years.
* **Temperature Increase Projections (IPCC AR6):**
    * **Under high emissions scenarios (SSP5-8.5):**  Global surface temperature could rise by 3.1°C (5.6°F) by 2100, leading to catastrophic and potentially irreversible changes.
    * **Under moderate emissions scenarios (SSP2-4.5):**  A temperature rise of 2.0°C (3.6°F) by 2100 is possible, with significant impacts.
* **Global Carbon Budget (World Resources Institute):** In 2022, approximately 36.8 billion metric tons of CO2 were emitted globally - significantly exceeding the remaining 1.5°C pathway limit.
* **Sea Level Rise Projections:**  The IPCC projects sea levels could rise by 0.39 – 0.86 meters (1.3 – 2.8 feet) by 2100, depending on emissions scenarios.

**III. Recent Developments (October 2023)**

* **Record Heat:** October 2023 has seen numerous record-breaking high temperatures across the globe, from Europe and North America to Asia and Africa.
* **Arctic Amplification:** The Arctic is warming at roughly twice the global average rate, with significant implications for sea ice loss and weather patterns.
* **UN Climate Change Conference (COP28 – Dubai):** Ongoing discussions focused on accelerating the transition to renewable energy, reducing emissions, and providing financial support to developing nations. Key topics include the global stocktake of progress towards the Paris Agreement goals.
* **Climate Litigation:**  Legal challenges against governments and corporations over climate change inaction are increasing worldwide.
* **Renewable Energy Growth:**  Solar and wind energy costs continue to decline, making them increasingly competitive with fossil fuels. Global renewable energy capacity is growing rapidly.

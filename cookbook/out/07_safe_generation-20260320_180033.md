```spl2
-- SPL 2.0: Safe Generation with Exception Handling
-- Demonstrates LLM-specific error handling patterns

WORKFLOW safe_generation
  INPUT: @prompt text
  OUTPUT: @result text
  SECURITY: CLASSIFICATION: internal
DO
  GENERATE response(@prompt) INTO @result

  -- Quality check via semantic evaluation (LLM-as-judge)
  GENERATE quality_assess(@result) INTO @quality

  EVALUATE @quality
    WHEN 'high_quality' THEN
      COMMIT @result WITH status = 'high_quality'
    WHEN 'acceptable' THEN
      GENERATE improved(@result, @prompt) INTO @result
      COMMIT @result WITH status = 'refined'
    OTHERWISE
      COMMIT @result WITH status = 'best_effort'
  END

EXCEPTION
  WHEN HallucinationDetected THEN
    GENERATE response(@prompt) INTO @result
    COMMIT @result WITH status = 'conservative'

  WHEN ContextLengthExceeded THEN
    RETRY WITH temperature = 0.1

  WHEN RefusalToAnswer THEN
    COMMIT @prompt WITH status = 'refused'

  WHEN BudgetExceeded THEN
    COMMIT @result WITH status = 'truncated'
END
```

```output
The internet is a complex network of interconnected computers and servers that communicate with each other using standardized protocols. Here's a simplified explanation of how it works:

**Hardware Components**

* Computers and devices connected to the internet (e.g., laptops, smartphones, smart home devices)
* Routers: specialized hardware that connect multiple networks together
* Switches: also known as routers in some contexts, they direct traffic between networks
* Internet Service Providers (ISPs): companies that provide internet access to users

**Network Architecture**

The internet is a decentralized network, meaning it's not controlled by a single entity. Instead, it's made up of many different networks, each managed by a separate organization. The most popular networks include:

1. Internet Protocol (IP) networks
2. Domain Name Systems (DNS)
3. Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol (TCP/IP)

**How Data is Transmitted**

Here's what happens when you send data over the internet:

1. **Your device sends a request**: When you enter a URL or send an email, your device breaks it down into smaller packets of data.
2. **Data is routed through routers**: The packet is passed through multiple routers, each one directing it to its next destination based on IP addresses and routing tables.
3. **Traffic is transmitted through the internet**: As the packet travels through the internet, it's transmitted over fiber-optic cables or wireless signals.
4. **Data is received by a server**: When the packet arrives at its final destination (e.g., a website), it's processed by the server and returned to your device as a complete response.

**Internet Protocols**

Two primary protocols govern how data is transmitted on the internet:

1. **TCP/IP**: Ensures reliable, error-checked communication between devices.
2. **HTTP/HTTPS**: Standard protocols for transferring data over the internet (e.g., web browsing).

**Additional Factors**

Some additional factors that affect internet performance include:

* Server load
* Network congestion
* Internet speed and latency
* Quality of Service (QoS) settings

That's a simplified overview of how the internet works!
```

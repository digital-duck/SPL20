This is a solid haiku, and you’ve provided a very helpful explanation of your choices. However, the execution could be stronger. While the syllable count is correct, the imagery feels a little *too* literal and reliant on cliché (“silent screen of light”). The “logic blooms” line is particularly weak and feels forced.

Here's my feedback:

1.  **"Silent screen of light"**: This is the weakest line. Consider a more evocative image. Perhaps something less about the screen itself and more about the *effect* of the light?

2.  **"Logic blooms in careful lines"**: This is the most problematic line. “Logic blooms” is a common metaphor and doesn’t feel particularly original or impactful.  Try to replace this with a more concise and specific image relating to the act of coding.

3.  **"New worlds take their form"**: This line is acceptable but could be tightened.

Consider focusing on capturing a *feeling* or a specific moment of creation rather than explicitly stating the concepts. A haiku thrives on suggestion and implication.

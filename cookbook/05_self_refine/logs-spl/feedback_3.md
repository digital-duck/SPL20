This is a good effort, and you’ve clearly addressed the previous feedback. However, the revision still feels a bit…sterile. The language remains somewhat detached and the imagery, while attempting to be evocative, isn't truly compelling. “Zeroes, ones ignite” is functional, but lacks a spark. “Code’s dark design blooms softly” is the weakest line – “dark design” is a cliché, and “softly” diminishes the power of creation. Finally, “Worlds begin to grow” is a common and somewhat tired metaphor.

Here's what needs work:

1.  **Specificity:** Haiku thrive on precise, sensory details. Instead of “Zeroes, ones ignite,” consider a more active verb or a detail that *shows* the ignition. For example, "Bits flash, then they burn."

2.  **Stronger Imagery:** "Code's dark design blooms softly" needs a sharper image. Can you replace "design" with something more specific to coding – algorithms, circuits, etc.?

3.  **Conciseness:** Haiku are notoriously difficult to execute well, and the 5-7-5 syllable structure is paramount. Review the syllable count of each line carefully.

4.  **Emotional Resonance:** What *feeling* are you trying to convey about coding? Is it the potential, the challenge, the beauty? Infuse the poem with that feeling.

I’m not saying this is *bad*, but it needs more focus, more vivid imagery, and a tighter adherence to the form. Don't just aim for “immediate” – aim for *impactful*.
